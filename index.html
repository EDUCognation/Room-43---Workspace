Sessions 1 - 3

Core Stance: Master the Hook

To write a strong persuasive essay, you must introduce your position with an engaging opening statement. In these initial sessions, we study the purpose of persuasive communication and how to organize concepts.

💡

What is a Perspective?

A perspective is your point of view. In persuasive writing, you articulate one clear side of an argument, using supporting details to help the reader understand your rationale.

Primary Objective: Inform, explain, and convince.

🍪 The OREO Framework

  • O
    Opinion: Clearly state your position on the topic.
  • R
    Reason: Give logical reasons that support your position.
  • E
    Explanation: Provide concrete examples, evidence, or data.
  • O
    Opinion: Reiterate your stance to leave a strong impression.

Model Essay

Read Carefully

Title: Why We Must Save Our Shared Cultures

Think of your family's favorite tradition or story. How would you feel if it vanished overnight, lost to history like a whisper in the wind? Across the globe, unique ancestral cultures are fading at an alarming rate. If we do not actively protect these traditions, we are risking the loss of humanity's shared memory.

Firstly, our heritage is our mirror. It teaches us where we come from and why we speak, think, or act in unique ways. Studies from global social researchers show that students who connect with their traditional languages show significantly higher self-esteem and resilience in school. Cultural pride builds a protective shield around vulnerable youth, guiding them forward with a deep sense of identity.

Secondly, unity cannot exist without diversity. When different viewpoints and cultures intertwine, communities become infinitely stronger, more creative, and more collaborative. Just as an ecosystem needs millions of diverse species to survive, a society needs diverse voices to solve complicated, modern problems.

Opponents might argue that holding onto old customs prevents modern societies from globalizing or moving forward. However, this is a dangerous error. True progress does not demand that we destroy our historical foundations. We can build soaring skyscrapers while still respecting the soil upon which they stand.

In summary, we stand at a critical crossroads. Will we let our languages, stories, and songs dissolve into a quiet void, or will we pass them on as shining lanterns for the youth? Let us speak up, share our unique roots, and fund local cultural centers before it is forever too late.
